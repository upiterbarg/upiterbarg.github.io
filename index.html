Hey there! I’m a rising senior studying course 18C at MIT.

About Me

I am broadly interested in applied probability and stochastic processes, as well as in the design of optimal decision-making schemes and uncertainty quantification methods for environments with complex dynamics.

Research

I am currently an undergraduate research assistant in Josh Tenenbaum’s lab, studying (1) the cognitive bases of strategy learning, (2) hierarchical reinforcement learning, and (3) graph neural networks algorithms for physical dynamics prediction.

This coming fall, I will be joining the Climate Modeling Alliance to work with Raffaele Ferrari on developing uncertainty quantification methods for parameterizations of stochastic dynamics in large-scale planetary flows as an MIT Center for Computational Science and Engineering Advanced Undergraduate Research Scholar.
